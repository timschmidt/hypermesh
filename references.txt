Here’s the condensed reference list I’d keep for **Hypermesh fast exact mesh booleans**, grouped by use.

## Highest-priority mesh boolean / arrangement papers

1. **Trettner, P.; Nehring-Wirxel, J.; Kobbelt, L.** “**EMBER: Exact Mesh Booleans via Efficient & Robust Local Arrangements**.” *ACM Transactions on Graphics* 41(4), SIGGRAPH 2022. doi:10.1145/3528223.3530181.
   Best fit for Hypermesh’s likely direction: exact local arrangements, homogeneous/integer-style constructions, adaptive subdivision, early-outs, parallelism. ([Graphics RWTH Aachen][1])

2. **Cherchi, G.; Pellacini, F.; Attene, M.; Livesu, M.** “**Interactive and Robust Mesh Booleans**.” *ACM Transactions on Graphics* 41(6), 2022, pp. 1–14. doi:10.1145/3550454.3555460.
   Strong practical performance reference for robust booleans at interactive scale. ([Unifind][2])

3. **Zhou, Q.; Grinspun, E.; Zorin, D.; Jacobson, A.** “**Mesh Arrangements for Solid Geometry**.” *ACM Transactions on Graphics* 35(4), SIGGRAPH 2016. doi:10.1145/2897824.2925901.
   Core arrangement + winding-vector formulation for robust mesh booleans. ([Columbia Computer Science][3])

4. **Lévy, B.** “**Exact Predicates, Exact Constructions and Combinatorics for Mesh CSG**.” arXiv:2405.12949, 2024.
   Especially relevant for exact co-refinement, Weiler model construction, radial sorting around non-manifold intersection edges, and CDT remeshing. ([arXiv][4])

5. **Barki, H.; Guennebaud, G.; Foufou, S.** “**Exact, Robust, and Efficient Regularized Booleans on General 3D Meshes**.” *Computers & Mathematics with Applications* 70(6), 2015, pp. 1235–1254. doi:10.1016/j.camwa.2015.06.016.
   Good reference for regularized booleans and degenerate closed/orientable mesh cases. ([ScienceDirect][5])

6. **Bernstein, G.; Fussell, D.** “**Fast, Exact, Linear Booleans**.” *Computer Graphics Forum* 28(5), 2009, pp. 1269–1278. doi:10.1111/j.1467-8659.2009.01504.x.
   Older but important BSP-style exact boolean reference with a small predicate substrate. ([Wiley Online Library][6])

7. **Nehring-Wirxel, J.; Trettner, P.; Kobbelt, L.** “**Fast Exact Booleans for Iterated CSG using Octree-Embedded BSPs**.” *Computer-Aided Design* 135, 2021, 103015. doi:10.1016/j.cad.2021.103015.
   Important if Hypermesh grows an iterated-CSG or plane/BSP backend. ([ScienceDirect][7])

8. **Hachenberger, P.; Kettner, L.; Mehlhorn, K.** “**Boolean Operations on 3D Selective Nef Complexes: Data Structure, Algorithms, Optimized Implementation and Experiments**.” *Computational Geometry* 38(1–2), 2007, pp. 64–99. doi:10.1016/j.comgeo.2006.11.009.
   Semantics reference for mixed-dimensional, non-manifold, open/closed, and regularized topology. ([ScienceDirect][8])

## Predicate / construction substrate

9. **Attene, M.** “**Indirect Predicates for Geometric Constructions**.” *Computer-Aided Design* 126, 2020, 102856. doi:10.1016/j.cad.2020.102856.
   Probably the most important subalgorithmic reference for Hypermesh: compare constructed intersection points through their source primitives instead of eagerly materializing exact coordinates. ([ScienceDirect][9])

10. **Shewchuk, J. R.** “**Adaptive Precision Floating-Point Arithmetic and Fast Robust Geometric Predicates**.” *Discrete & Computational Geometry* 18(3), 1997, pp. 305–363. doi:10.1007/PL00009321.
    Foundational adaptive exact predicate work. ([CiNii Research][10])

11. **Yap, C. K.** “**Towards Exact Geometric Computation**.” *Computational Geometry* 7(1–2), 1997, pp. 3–23. doi:10.1016/0925-7721(95)00040-2.
    Foundational exact geometric computation framing. ([ScienceDirect][11])

12. **Guigue, P.; Devillers, O.** “**Fast and Robust Triangle-Triangle Overlap Test Using Orientation Predicates**.” *Journal of Graphics Tools* 8(1), 2003, pp. 25–32. doi:10.1080/10867651.2003.10487580.
    Good fit for `hyperlimit`-style orientation-only triangle overlap decisions. ([Taylor & Francis Online][12])

13. **Möller, T.** “**A Fast Triangle-Triangle Intersection Test**.” *Journal of Graphics Tools* 2(2), 1997, pp. 25–30. doi:10.1080/10867651.1997.10487472.
    Classic fast triangle-triangle staging reference. ([Taylor & Francis Online][13])

14. **Garau, L.; Cherchi, G.** “**A Robust Approach to Detect Intersections between Triangles with Different Numerical Representations**.” arXiv:2507.08478, 2025.
    Newer and relevant because Hypermesh/Hyperreal may need predicates across exact, rational, floating, and implicit representations. ([arXiv][14])

## Broad phase / spatial indexing

15. **Klosowski, J. T.; Held, M.; Mitchell, J. S. B.; Sowizral, H.; Zikan, K.** “**Efficient Collision Detection Using Bounding Volume Hierarchies of k-DOPs**.” *IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics* 4(1), 1998, pp. 21–36. doi:10.1109/2945.675649.
    Best classic k-DOP/BVH reference; maps well to Hyper’s support-DOP direction. ([SUNY Research Connect][15])

16. **Xu, S.; Keyser, J.** “**Fast and Robust Booleans on Polyhedra**.” *Computer-Aided Design* 45(2), 2013, pp. 529–534. doi:10.1016/j.cad.2012.10.036.
    Useful for broad-phase spatial hashing + exact topology / approximate geometry separation. ([ScienceDirect][16])

## Planar arrangements / triangulation support

17. **Held, M.** “**FIST: Fast Industrial-Strength Triangulation of Polygons**.” *Algorithmica* 30(4), 2001, pp. 563–596. doi:10.1007/s00453-001-0028-4.
    Useful for practical earcut-style polygon triangulation behavior. ([ResearchGate][17])

18. **Boissonnat, J.-D.; Devillers, O.; Pion, S.; Teillaud, M.; Yvinec, M.** “**Triangulations in CGAL**.” *Computational Geometry* 22(1–3), 2002, pp. 5–19. doi:10.1016/S0925-7721(01)00054-2.
    Good implementation reference for robust triangulation package design. ([ScienceDirect][18])

19. **Lee, D. T.; Lin, A. K.** “**Generalized Delaunay Triangulation for Planar Graphs**.” *Discrete & Computational Geometry* 1, 1986, pp. 201–218.
    Relevant to constrained/generalized Delaunay topology for face-cell remeshing. ([EuDML][19])

## Practical baselines / libraries to inspect

20. **CGAL Polygon Mesh Processing: Corefinement and Boolean Operations.**
    Corefinement baseline: refine both meshes so intersection polylines become edges, then compute union/intersection/difference. ([CGAL 6.2 Manual][20])

21. **CGAL 3D Nef Polyhedra.**
    Baseline for fully general boolean/topological semantics, including open/closed sets, mixed-dimensional cells, non-manifold cases, closure/interior/boundary operations. ([CGAL 6.2 Manual][21])

22. **Manifold Library.**
    Practical robust manifold-output boolean implementation; useful as an engineering comparison even though it is not the same exact-arithmetic philosophy as Hypermesh. ([GitHub][22])

23. **Solidean / EMBER-derived production kernel.**
    Commercial/production descendant of the EMBER line; useful mainly as a signal for engineering choices around exact predicates/constructions, fixed-width arithmetic, attributes, and production throughput. ([solidean.com][23])
